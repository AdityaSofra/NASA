What Does NASA Do?

At NASA, we explore space and conduct scientific research to advance human understanding of the universe. Our work includes sending missions to other planets, studying Earth from space, developing cutting-edge technologies, and improving aviation. We strive to inspire the next generation through STEM education and public engagement, driving innovations that benefit society and push the boundaries of what is possible.

The Launch of Goes-U

On Monday, June 24, NASA and Space gave the 'go' for the launch of the GOES-U weather satellite mission following a successful readiness review. The two-hour launch window opens at 5:16 p.m. EDT on Tuesday, June 25.

Earth Information Center

For more than 50 years, NASA satellites have provided data on Earth's land, water, air, temperature, and climate. NASA's Earth Information Center allows visitors to see how our planet is changing in six key areas: sea level rise and coastal impacts, health and air quality, wildfires, greenhouse gases, sustainable energy, and agriculture.
